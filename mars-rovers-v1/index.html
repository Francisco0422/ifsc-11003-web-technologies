Welcome to Ford

For more than a century, Ford Motor Company has stood as a symbol of innovation, strength, and progress. What began in 1903 with Henry Ford’s dream to make transportation accessible to all has grown into a global movement driving the future of mobility. At Ford, our mission is to build a better world where every person is free to move and pursue their dreams. We’re dedicated to creating innovative, high-quality vehicles and mobility solutions that make life easier, safer, and more sustainable for everyone. Driven by our passion for progress, we strive to shape the future of transportation through smart technology, environmental responsibility, and a deep commitment to our customers and communities worldwide.

Vehicles

Ford F-150

  • Powerful Performance: The F-150 offers a range of strong engines, including gas, hybrid, and electric options, with top models towing up to 14,000 pounds.
  • Advanced Technology: It features Ford Co-Pilot360™ safety systems and the SYNC® 4 infotainment system with smart connectivity and voice control.
  • Durable Design: Built with a military-grade aluminum-alloy body and a high-strength steel frame, the F-150 is tough, efficient, and built to last.

Ford Mustang GTD

  • Thrilling Performance: Powered by a supercharged 5.2-liter V8 engine, the Mustang GTD delivers an astounding 815 horsepower and 664 lb-ft of torque, making it the most powerful production Mustang ever built.
  • Track-Focused Engineering: Engineered for precision and performance, it features a rear-mounted 8-speed dual-clutch transaxle, carbon-fiber body panels, active aerodynamics (including a drag-reduction system), and pushrod suspension — combining to deliver supercar-level handling on and off the track.
  • Elite Construction & Limited Production: Handcrafted in partnership with Multimatic, the Mustang GTD is built using lightweight materials such as carbon fiber and magnesium wheels. Produced in limited numbers, this exclusive supercar is expected to start around USD $300,000, representing the pinnacle of Ford’s performance engineering.
  • Racing Legend: The Ford GT40 was created in the 1960s to challenge Ferrari at Le Mans, famously winning the 24 Hours of Le Mans four consecutive times from 1966 to 1969.
  • Power and Design: It featured a mid-mounted V8 engine and an aerodynamic, low-slung body — the “40” in its name refers to its 40-inch height.
  • Historic Legacy: The GT40 became an icon of American engineering and endurance racing, inspiring modern successors like the Ford GT and Mustang GTD.

Ford's Background

Ford Motor Company was founded on June 16, 1903, by Henry Ford and a group of investors in Detroit, Michigan. With a vision to make transportation accessible to everyone, Ford revolutionized the automotive industry by introducing assembly line production in 1913, dramatically reducing manufacturing time and costs. This innovation made the Model T, launched in 1908, affordable for the average American and transformed personal mobility across the world. Over the decades, Ford became a symbol of innovation, quality, and strength, expanding its lineup to include trucks, SUVs, and performance vehicles like the Mustang and F-150. The company also played a crucial role in industrial and technological development during the 20th century, including contributions to aviation and military production during wartime. Today, Ford continues to evolve, leading the shift toward electric and hybrid vehicles, smart mobility solutions, and sustainability initiatives. Guided by its mission to “build a better world where every person is free to move and pursue their dreams,” Ford remains one of the most influential automakers in history—combining its rich heritage with a forward-looking commitment to innovation, quality, and community.
